Coulomb's Law
\[ \mathbf{F}_{12} = \dfrac{q_1 q_2 (\mathbf{r}_1 - \mathbf{r}_2)}{4 \pi \varepsilon \left| \mathbf{r}_1 - \mathbf{r}_2 \right|^3} = \dfrac{q_1 q_2 \mathbf{\hat{r}}_{12}}{4 \pi \varepsilon \left| \mathbf{\hat{r}}_{12} \right|^2} \]
The electrostatic force exerted by a charge \( q_1 \) located at \( \mathbf{r_1} \) on another charge \( q_2 \) located at \( \mathbf{r_2} \). The line of force is specified by the unit vector \( \mathbf{\hat{r}}_{12} \), and the direction depends on the charges (like charges repel, opposite charges attract). This force follows an inverse-square law, where \( F \propto 1 / r^2 \). The force is also modulated by the medium's electrical permittivity \( \varepsilon = \varepsilon_0 \varepsilon_r \).
